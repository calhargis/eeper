# eeper Trademark Policy

The eeper source code is free software (see LICENSING.md). The **"eeper" name and logo are not** — they are held by the project to protect users, and this policy states what uses are and aren't okay. The concern is specific: eeper is a baby-monitoring project with a deliberate safety stance, and the harm we're guarding against is a modified or degraded fork shipping _under our name_, trading on our reputation while shedding our safety posture.

## You may, without asking

- Use the name to truthfully refer to the project ("works with eeper", "an eeper sensor node", "forked from eeper").
- Distribute unmodified builds of eeper under the eeper name.
- Write articles, tutorials, videos, and reference builds using the name.
- Use the name in package identifiers where convention requires it (e.g., `eeper-client` for an API client), provided the package doesn't claim to be the official project.

## You may not, without written permission

- Ship a **modified** version under the name "eeper" or a confusingly similar name (eeperPro, eeper+, eeper Cloud). Forks must rebrand. (Patches carried by distro packagers for packaging purposes don't count as modifications for this clause.)
- Use the name or logo in a way that implies official status, endorsement, or affiliation — especially for hosted services, hardware products, or anything sold.
- Use the name on any product or service that makes medical, diagnostic, or vital-sign claims. This use is never granted.

## Notes

- Naming rights and the safety stance travel together: the project will not authorize the mark for uses that contradict docs/MASTER_PLAN.md §2.
- This policy is modeled on common open source norms (cf. the policies of Debian-adjacent and CNCF projects) and is intended to be enforced with proportionality — a friendly email first.
- Open item: formal trademark registration for "eeper" is tracked in the master plan's open questions; this policy applies to our common-law rights in the meantime.

Questions or permission requests: open an issue titled "trademark:" or contact the maintainers.
